Rebellion in the Colonies


Ninety-Two Resolutions were a list of complaints and demands for political reform in Lower Canada. When Britain rejected them, it increased tensions and helped spark rebellion.

The Ninety-Two Resolutions included a long list of complaints and requests.

Key demands were:
- More power for the elected Legislative Assembly
- Greater control over government finances
- An elected Legislative Council (instead of appointed elites)
- More representation for French Canadians
