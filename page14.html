Upper Canada

3.) Protestant religious influence:
Upper Canada had a largely Protestant population, especially members of the Anglican Church also known as The Church of England.

4.) Farming Communities
Most of the settlers lived in rural communities and depended on farming as a way of life.

5.)Limited democratic power
Upper Canada had an elected assembly, but much of the real political power remained with British-appointed officials and later elites such as the Family Compact.
