Family Compact


The Family Compact was a small and powerful group of wealthy landowners, government officials, and members of the Anglican Church who controlled much of the political and economic power in Upper Canada in the early 1800s.

The political system that allowed this group to gain influence was created by the Constitutional Act of 1791, which established the colony’s government.

The Family Compact generally supported strong ties to Britain, the authority of the Anglican Church, and the continuation of elite control over political decisions. They often opposed democratic reforms that would give more power to the elected Legislative Assembly.
