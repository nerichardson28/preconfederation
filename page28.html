Rebellion in the Colonies


Louis-Joseph Papineau
-was a French-Canadian lawyer, seigneur, and political reformer who became the foremost leader of the Patriote movement in Lower Canada
-Papineau drafted the 92 Resolutions of 1834, a sweeping list of grievances and demands for reform. The British rejection of these led to widespread protest. Though personally cautious, his Patriote movement sparked the armed uprisings of 1837–1838.
Facing arrest after the Battle of Saint-Charles, he fled to the United States and then France, publishing essays on the insurrection while in exile
