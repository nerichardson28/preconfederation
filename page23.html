Structure of Government in Lower Canada


1. The Governor
The governor was appointed by Britain and represented the British Crown in the colony. The governor had significant power, including the authority to approve or reject laws passed by the colonial assembly.

2. The Legislative Council
The Legislative Council was made up of members appointed by the governor. These members were often wealthy merchants or influential individuals. The council reviewed and approved laws before they could take effect.

3. The Legislative Assembly
The Legislative Assembly was the only part of the government that was elected by the people. Most members of this assembly were French Canadians, since they made up the majority of the population.
