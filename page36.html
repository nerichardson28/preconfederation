Lord Durham's Report


But What is Responsible Government?

Responsible government is a parliamentary system where the executive branch (Prime Minister/Premier and Cabinet) depends on the support of an elected legislative assembly, rather than a monarch or appointed officials, to remain in power. It ensures the government is accountable to citizens through their elected representatives.

This would be possible if the executive (or in modern terms, the Cabinet) was drawn from and held the support of the majority in the elected assembly. Such a reform would reduce the power of the Family Compact and the Chateau Clique and give more power to the Legislative Assembly such as what Louis-Joseph Papineau and William Lyon Mackenzie tried to achieve through their acts of rebellion.
