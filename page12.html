Lower Canada

3.) Seigneurial land system:
Lower Canada kept the Seigneurial system, where land was divided into long narrow strips along rivers. Farmers rented land from seigneurs (landowners).

4.) French civil law and traditions:
Even though Britain controlled the colony, many French legal and cultural traditions were maintained, including French civil law.

5.) Limited political power for most colonists
Although Lower Canada had an elected assembly after the Constitutional Act of 1791, real power often remained with British officials and wealthy elites like the Château Clique.
