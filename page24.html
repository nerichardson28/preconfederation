Chateau Clique

The Château Clique was a small but powerful group of wealthy merchants, landowners, and government officials who held significant influence in Lower Canada after the political system created by the Constitutional Act of 1791.

The Château Clique was made up mostly of English-speaking merchants and business leaders, many of whom lived in cities like Quebec and Montreal. Because they controlled important political and economic positions, they were able to influence government decisions, trade policies, and land development.
