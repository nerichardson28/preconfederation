Return to the Seigneurial System

The land was usually divided into long, narrow strips that stretched back from rivers such as the St. Lawrence River. This layout allowed many farmers to have access to water for transportation, irrigation, and daily use.

By 1791, most farmers in Lower Canada were still living under this system. It shaped the agriculture, settlement patterns, and social structure of the colony. While it was different from the land ownership system used in Upper Canada, the British government allowed it to continue in Lower Canada in order to respect French traditions and maintain stability among the French-speaking population.
