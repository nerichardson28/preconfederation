Responsibilities in the Community

Responsibilities of the Colonial Governor

1.) Represent the British Crown
The governor was the highest official in the colony and represented the authority of the British government and monarch.

2.) Grant land to seigneurs
The governor had the authority to approve or grant large sections of land (seigneurs) to seigneurs so that they could organize settlement and farming.

3.) Oversee colonial administration
The governor surpervised how the colony was governed, ensuring the laws and policies were followed.

4.) Maintain order and stability
The governor worked to keep the peace in the colony and manage relationships between different groups, including settlers, seigneurs, and Indigenous peoples.

5.) Ensure the system supported settlement
By overseeing land distribution and administration, the governor helped make sure the seigneurial system encouraged settlment and economic development.
