The British Answer: Send Lord Durham


John George Lambton, the Earl of Durham, was a British political reformer.
He was appointed governor general of British North America by the imperial Prime Minister, Lord Melbourne.
Durham was asked to investigate colonial grievances after the rebellions of 1837–38 in Upper and Lower Canada.
He arrived in Canada in May 1838. However, he had a dispute with the government in London and resigned his appointment four months later.
He returned to Britain.
In 1839, he completed his Report on the Affairs of British North America.
