Structure of Government in Upper Canada


The government in Upper Canada had three main parts:

1. The Governor
The governor was appointed by the British Crown and represented British authority in the colony. The governor had significant power, including the ability to approve or reject laws and influence government decisions.

2. The Legislative Council
The Legislative Council was made up of members appointed by the governor. These individuals were usually wealthy landowners or influential officials. The council reviewed laws proposed by the Legislative Assembly.

3. The Legislative Assembly
The Legislative Assembly was the only part of the government elected by colonists. Its members represented local communities and proposed laws, but they had limited power because the governor and Legislative Council could reject their decisions.
